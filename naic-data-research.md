### State Minimum Requirements

**IMPORTANT: Minimums changed effective July 1, 2025 (S.L. 2023-133, as amended by S.L. 2024-29)**

Before July 1, 2025:
- Bodily Injury: $30,000 per person / $60,000 per accident
- Property Damage: $25,000
- Uninsured Motorist: Required — same as liability minimums

**Effective July 1, 2025 (for new policies or renewals on/after that date):**
- Bodily Injury: $50,000 per person / $100,000 per accident
- Property Damage: $50,000
- Uninsured Motorist: Required — matched to BI/PD limits
- Underinsured Motorist: Now included in all new/renewed policies

- PIP: Not required (at-fault state; Medical Payments is optional)

Sources: https://www.ncdot.gov/dmv/title-registration/insurance-requirements/Pages/default.aspx ; https://www.ncdoi.gov/changes-rating-automobile-insurance-policies-effective-july-1-2025  
Statutes: G.S. 20-309 (compulsory insurance); G.S. 20-279.21 (minimum coverage amounts and UM requirement) — https://www.ncleg.net/enactedlegislation/statutes/html/bysection/chapter_20/gs_20-279.21.html ; S.L. 2023-133 / S.L. 2024-29 (July 2025 changes)

Note: Michigan ranked 8th nationally. Only 0.6% increase from 2021 ($1,311.62) — reflects impact of 2020 no-fault reform. 2022 data is post-reform.

### State Minimum Requirements

Michigan operates under a unique no-fault system (MCL 500.3009 and related provisions, amended July 2020):

**Three required coverages:**

1. **Personal Injury Protection (PIP):** Required — driver chooses level:
   - Unlimited (default if no selection made)
   - $500,000 per person per accident
   - $250,000 per person per accident
   - $50,000 per person (minimum available, with restrictions)
   - Opt-out available only if covered by qualifying health insurance

2. **Property Protection Insurance (PPI):** Required — $1,000,000 (fixed by statute; pays for damage to others' property in Michigan)

3. **Residual Bodily Injury / Property Damage Liability (BI/PD):**
   - Minimum purchasable: $50,000 per person / $100,000 per accident (requires written election)
   - Default (if no selection): $250,000 per person / $500,000 per accident
   - Out-of-state property damage: $10,000
   - Prior to July 2, 2020 minimums were $20,000/$40,000

- Uninsured Motorist: Optional (not required under Michigan no-fault law)

Sources: https://www.michigan.gov/autoinsurance/frequently-asked-questions ; https://www.legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=mcl-500-3009  
Statutes: MCL 500.3009 (BI/PD minimum limits); MCL 500.3101 et seq. (no-fault requirements)

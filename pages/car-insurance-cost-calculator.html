How This Calculator Works

This calculator starts with national average car insurance data and adjusts based on several key factors:

  • Age: Drivers under 25 pay significantly more (up to 2.5x the average). Rates decrease through adulthood and may increase slightly after age 70.
  • State: State averages range from about $1,180 (Maine) to $3,096 (Michigan) for full coverage.
  • Driving record: At-fault accidents increase premiums 30%–50%. A DUI can double or triple rates.
  • Credit score: Drivers with poor credit pay 65%–115% more than those with excellent credit in most states.
  • Vehicle type: Sports and luxury vehicles cost 15%–40% more to insure than standard sedans. Economy cars are often the cheapest.
  • Coverage level: Full coverage costs approximately 2.5x to 3x more than state minimum liability-only coverage.

What to Do After Getting Your Estimate

  1. Get actual quotes — Use this estimate as a benchmark, then get real quotes from at least 3–5 insurance companies to find the best rate for your specific situation.
  2. Ask about discounts — Make sure to inquire about all available discounts: multi-policy, safe driver, good student, defensive driving, low mileage, and more.
  3. Consider your deductible — A higher deductible lowers your premium. Choose the highest amount you can comfortably afford to pay out of pocket.
  4. Review coverage needs — Make sure you have enough liability coverage to protect your assets. Most experts recommend at least 100/300/100.

Frequently Asked Questions

This calculator provides estimates based on national average data and industry-standard rating factors. It is designed to give you a general idea of what you might expect to pay. Actual quotes from insurance companies will differ because they use additional factors not captured here, including your exact zip code, specific vehicle year/make/model, exact driving history, and proprietary credit-based insurance scores. Always get actual quotes from multiple insurers for the most accurate pricing.

Each insurance company uses its own proprietary models to assess risk and set prices. They weigh factors differently — one company might place more emphasis on credit score, while another prioritizes driving record or vehicle type. They also have different overhead costs, profit targets, and claims experiences. This is why comparing quotes from multiple companies is the single most important thing you can do to save money on car insurance. Rates for the same driver can vary by 50% or more between companies.
